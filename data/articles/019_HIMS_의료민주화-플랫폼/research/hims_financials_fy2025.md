## 10. Verdict: Is Marketing Eating the Profits?

**Nuanced answer: Marketing is NOT the primary margin killer. Gross margin compression is.**

### The real story in 3 points:

1. **Marketing efficiency improved materially.** 46% → 39% of revenue. Marketing grew only 35% vs 59% revenue growth. This is genuine operating leverage.

2. **Gross margin collapse is the problem.** 79.5% → 73.8% full year, and Q4 hit 71.9%. COGS grew 102.5% on 59% revenue growth. The product mix is shifting toward lower-margin categories (weight loss, international, personalized treatments).

3. **The opex BELOW marketing is growing fast.** Tech +89%, G&A +63%, Ops +54%. These offset the marketing leverage. The company is investing in infrastructure, international headcount, and engineering talent — all of which management calls "investment period" spending.

### The math:
- Marketing leverage saved ~6.8pp of revenue = ~$160M
- But gross margin erosion cost ~5.6pp = ~$131M
- And Tech/G&A growth cost ~1.3pp = ~$31M
- Net improvement: only +0.3pp operating margin

### Forward risk:
- Q1 2026 guided to 7% EBITDA margin (Super Bowl + weight loss cadence)
- FY2026 EBITDA midpoint implies 12% margin (vs 13.5% in FY2025 = regression)
- Subscriber growth decelerating to ~13% YoY
- Must prove weight loss / international can scale without further GM erosion
